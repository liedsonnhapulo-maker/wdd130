2.5 Video Submission: Perception

  • Due Mar 14 by 11:59pm
  • Points 20
  • Submitting a text entry box, a media recording, or a file upload
  • Attempts 1
  • Allowed Attempts 3
  • Available until Mar 28 at 11:59pm

"Strength lies in differences, not similarities" (Stephen R. Covey).

Overview

Living in a diverse world can sometimes be compared to dancing on a tightrope, where we have to celebrate differences on one end and delicately negotiate conflicting viewpoints on the other. This delicate balance can often lead to missteps as we seek to understand another's viewpoint that is different than our own. One of the biggest difficulties in ethical and perceptual discussions boils down to this main question: How can we maintain strong, personal beliefs and live harmoniously with those who see the world differently than we do? Consider the following counsel from President Dallin H. Oaks from his talk Loving Others and Living With Differences Links to an external site.:

"Followers of Christ should be examples of civility. We should love all people, be good listeners, and show concern for their sincere beliefs. Though we may disagree, we should not be disagreeable. Our stands and communications on controversial topics should not be contentious. We should be wise in explaining and pursuing our positions and in exercising our influence. In doing so, we ask that others not be offended by our sincere religious beliefs and the free exercise of our religion. We encourage all of us to practice the Savior’s Golden Rule: 'Whatsoever ye would that men should do to you, do ye even so to them' (Matthew 7:12). Links to an external site."

In this assignment, you will express your thoughts in a well-crafted video submission. This exercise is not meant to be a YouTube short or a TikTok short-form video but a carefully thought-out delivery with an introduction, relevant facts, and a solid conclusion—all fit in a tight timeline. You will be graded on how well you take the question and articulate your response cohesively in your own words. There is information in the Student Resources module that can help you with writing good paragraphs. This information can help translate your ideas into words that you would deliver on camera.

Please take time to review the following grading parameters for this assignment. The grading rubric at the end of this assignment will give you a complete understanding of how your video will be graded. 

Target Time: Two to Three Minutes

  • Your videos must be recorded in or uploaded directly to Canvas via the Record/Upload Media tool located on the Rich Content Editor toolbar of Canvas. Because Canvas restricts the data and size of uploaded videos, your submissions cannot exceed this time length. Rehearsing your video recording a few times and referencing a script are a few best practices to help ensure your finished video will comply with the allotted time limit. 
  • Be aware of the time constraints. Any content recorded past the three-minute mark will not be graded.

Recording Options

  • There are various options available to you to record your video. QuickTime, Zoom, Loom, and Microsoft Stream are the most user-friendly external software programs. You are also welcome to use your phone; however, be aware that some phone carriers record videos using uncommon video codecs, so you may need to use conversion software to get into a more user-friendly version compatible with Canvas.
  • The other option is for you to record directly to Canvas using the Record/Upload Media tool. This option enables you to use your computer as the recording device and record yourself directly to Canvas.  

Format

  • Introduction: Your video should include one introductory sentence that establishes what you intend to accomplish in the video. It should be brief, concise, and articulate the main focus of your message. This is your best chance to hook the viewer's attention, so be creative! Craft your opening statement in a way that encourages the viewer to continue watching your video. 
  • Body or development: This commentary is your opportunity to develop your story or argument. Because your time is limited, you need to consider the one or two main points you want to articulate and find the relevant resource that justifies your claim. You will typically be asked to comment or reference something from the assignment, so be sure to annotate this in your commentary as you prepare your presentation. The body and development should flow freely from the introduction sentence and not be a collection of random thoughts. 
  • Conclusion: The conclusion ties all the key points together and summarizes your message. In this, you should return to the introduction sentence and remind the viewer of your introduction and how the conclusion finalizes all of your arguments. End with a powerful thought that makes the viewer ponder and reflect on the most important takeaways from your video. 

Instructions

  1. Read or listen to Loving Others and Living With Differences Links to an external site..
  2. Choose one option to respond to for your video submission. You have two sets of questions to choose from that all revolve around this central theme of perception. Of the two options, select one option to respond to and craft your video. In your commentary, please include one quote or reference from President Oaks's talk that helped you form your analysis. Include this as part of your commentary.    
    • Option 1: What specific cultural, political, or gender issues around you seem to be met with a lack of civility? How have you been judged for your beliefs about politics, gender, or culture? What can be done to empower yourself in these situations instead of moving into a victim role? 
    • Option 2: What barriers do you have, based on your personal perceptions, when communicating with others who see the world differently? Describe using a real-world example. What will you do specifically to communicate your views more effectively with those who see the world differently than you do?
  3. Upload your video to Canvas. Your video must be uploaded directly to Canvas using the "Record/Upload" tool. To learn how to embed your video, watch this brief tutorial Download watch this brief tutorial (03:12).
